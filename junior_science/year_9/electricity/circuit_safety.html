Circuit Safety

Essential Safety Guidelines for Practical Labs

Year 9 Science

Mandatory Safety

You must wear Safety Glasses.

Put them on before touching any equipment.
Keep them on until everything is packed away.

The Golden Rule

Power is ALWAYS connected last.

Build your circuit with the power supply switched OFF.
Check your wires twice before turning it on.

Short Circuits

Never connect a wire directly from Positive (+) to Negative (-).

Electricity must always pass through a load, like a bulb.

The Danger

Short circuits make wires heat up instantly.

This melts plastic, ruins equipment, and causes severe skin burns.

Signs of Danger

  • Heat: A wire or battery feels hot to touch.
  • Smell: The scent of hot metal or burning plastic.
  • Symptom: Bulbs turn off or dim unexpectedly.

Power Supplies

Use the DC Terminals only.

Usually marked with Red (+) and Black (-).
Do not touch the AC terminals.

Keep Voltage Low

Only use settings between 2V and 4V.

Anything higher will instantly blow the bulbs and melt components.

No Liquids

Hands must be completely dry.

All water bottles must stay inside zipped school bags.

A Safe Desk

Keep bags and coats under the desk.

Avoid cluttered workbenches so you can trace your wires easily.

Emergency!

1. Switch Off

Immediately turn off the power pack at the source if safe to do so.

2. Tell the Teacher

Alert your teacher immediately. Do not try to move hot components yourself.

Final Checklist

  • ✓ Safety glasses are on.
  • ✓ Power pack is turned off.
  • ✓ Workbench is clear of drinks.
You are ready to build.
